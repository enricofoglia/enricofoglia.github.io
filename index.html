About Me

Ciao, thank you for passing by! I'm Enrico, and I am currently a PhD candidate at ISAE-Supaéro (France) and Université de Sherbrooke (Canada), under the supervision of Michael Bauerheim, Thierry Jardin and Stéphane Moreau.

We live in a sonic world that is getting more and more polluted: this is not only a matter of annoyance, but a public health and social justice issue! However, taking noise generation into account during numerical optimization cycles can be challenging due to the prohibitive cost of high fidelity simulations. In our group we want to tackle this issue by leveraging modern advances in statistical modeling, notably Neural Networks, and combining it with Bayesian Optimization to keep the required number for simulations as low as possible.

Publications

Towards the optimization of propeller tonal noise in the frequency domain using the discrete adjoint method

E. Foglia, M. Daroukh, M. Buszyk, I. Salah el-Din, and S. Moreau

Numerical optimization is, today, a challenging task in aeroacoustics because of the high number of simulations that it requires. The discrete adjoint drastically reduces this burden, at the cost of simulating an indirect solution that requires the knowledge of the gradients of the solver and of the objective function. We present a methodology that allows for the use of a wide range of acoustic functions, such as the source power level or the radiated sound pressure level at multiple observers, based on Hanson’s frequency domain formula for (steady) loading and thickness noise. The calculation of the forward and adjoint solutions is carried out using the industrial code elsA, coupled with a python implementation of the Hanson’s formula. The gradients of the discrete objective functions with respect to the grid coordinates and the flow variables have have been computed analytically, allowing for a very eﬀicient implementation directly in python. The overall the methodology has been thoroughly validated using existing tools and finite difference approximations of the gradients.
