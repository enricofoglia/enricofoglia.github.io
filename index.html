About

Ciao, thank you for passing by! I'm Enrico, and I am currently a PhD candidate at ISAE-Supaéro (France) and Université de Sherbrooke (Canada), under the supervision of Michael Bauerheim, Thierry Jardin and Stéphane Moreau.

We live in a sonic world that is getting more and more polluted: this is not only a matter of annoyance, but a public health and social justice issue. However, taking noise generation into account during numerical optimization cycles can be challenging due to the prohibitive cost of high-fidelity simulations. In our group we want to tackle this issue by leveraging modern advances in statistical modeling, notably neural networks, and combining it with Bayesian optimization to keep the required number of simulations as low as possible.

Publications

Towards the optimization of propeller tonal noise in the frequency domain using the discrete adjoint method

E. Foglia, M. Daroukh, M. Buszyk, I. Salah el-Din, and S. Moreau

Numerically optimizing rotors targeting their noise emission is challenging because computing the gradient can be extremely expensive. By leveraging the adjoint of the linearized Navier–Stokes equations, however, it is possible to compute the sensitivity of an objective function to an arbitrary number of parameters with a single computation. In this paper, we derive analytically the linearization of the acoustic pressure generated by a rotating blade, so that it can be used in any adjoint solver to take acoustics into account.
