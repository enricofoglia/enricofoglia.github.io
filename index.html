About Me

Ciao, thank you for passing by! I'm Enrico, and I am currently a PhD candidate at ISAE-Supaéro (France) and Université de Sherbrooke (Canada), under the supervision of Michael Bauerheim, Thierry Jardin and Stéphane Moreau.

We live in a sonic world that is getting more and more polluted: this is not only a matter of annoyance, but a public health and social justice issue! However, taking noise generation into account during numerical optimization cycles can be challenging due to the prohibitive cost of high fidelity simulations. In our group we want to tackle this issue by leveraging modern advances in statistical modeling, notably Neural Networks, and combining it with Bayesian Optimization to keep the required number for simulations as low as possible.

Previously I spent five months within the aeroacoustics group at ONERA (Paris), and helped develop a discrete adjoint solver for rotor noise in the frequency domain. This work was done under the supervision of Majd Daroukh, Martin Buszik and Itham Salah el-Din.

Feel free to contact me regarding papers I wrote, papers you might want to write with me, papers you think might be interesting for me or even just if you have a cool South American novel to suggest. In fact, probably novels should be number one in the list.
