Uncertainty quantification: teach neural nets to doubt themselves

Published on November 25, 2024 | 5 min read

In my research, I deal with a peculiar mix of aeroacoustics, which is the science of predicting sound produced by unsteady aerodynamics, and deep learning. Crucially, aeroacoustics is extremely delicate, and either needs very high-fidelity simulations or precise experiments to be investigated. This means, that any dataset about aeroacoustics is, by default, going to be quite limited in terms of datapoints. The question is: How can one plan new experiments (numerical or physical) by taking into account what the model does not know yet?

The general field that studies questions like this is called Uncertainty Quantification (UQ).
